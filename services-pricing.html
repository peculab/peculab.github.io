Pay for a defined decision, evidence, and handoff.

Each engagement states the business unit, use case, core team, data and integration assumptions, review cadence, deliverables, and exclusions. Final proposals are fixed to scope—not an open-ended block of consulting hours.

Recommended engagement path

One use case at a time, with a decision at every stage.

A stage may be purchased independently when the required inputs already exist. Moving to the next stage requires a new or amended statement of work.

Stage 1 · 1–2 weeks · one business unitUS$3,500 fixed fee

POC Readiness & Scope

For an organization with a promising AI idea that needs a defensible use case and test plan before building.

Base scope

  • One prioritized workflow or use case
  • One client sponsor and a core working group of up to 5 people
  • Review of the current workflow and up to 2 representative data sources or sample sets
  • Stakeholder discovery, risk discussion, and readiness review

Deliverables

  • Current-state workflow and opportunity map
  • Prioritized use-case statement and user need
  • Data-readiness, privacy, risk, and human-review checklist
  • Success measures, acceptance criteria, and POC backlog
  • Written go/no-go brief with recommended next step

Not included: prototype development, production architecture, legal or regulatory opinion, security testing, data cleaning, or implementation. Additional business units, use cases, or major datasets require a revised scope.

Stage 2 · 4–6 weeks · one managed POCUS$12,000–20,000

Managed AI Proof of Concept

For testing one workflow with limited representative data and a decision-grade prototype—not launching a production system.

Base scope

  • One use case, one business unit, and one prototype environment
  • Core client team of 3–8; one accountable sponsor
  • Typically up to 2 representative data sources and 1 limited integration or configured workflow
  • Weekly working review, decision log, and agreed feedback cycle

Deliverables

  • Confirmed baseline, test plan, acceptance criteria, and risk controls
  • Limited prototype or configured no-code/low-code workflow
  • Representative-data evaluation, error examples, and documented limitations
  • User walkthrough and operating notes
  • Evidence report and recommendation: stop, revise, pilot, or prepare for production engineering

Price range: the lower end assumes clean representative data and minimal integration; complexity, sensitive data, custom code, additional models, or more integrations moves the scope upward. Production deployment, continuous monitoring, security certification, and ongoing maintenance are not included.

Pilot & Adoption Leadership

For an organization with a validated concept that needs controlled real-world testing and accountable adoption support.

Base scope assumption

  • One business unit and one defined pilot workflow
  • One core delivery team of up to 8 people
  • One pilot user group of up to 25 users
  • Existing technical team or vendor remains responsible for production engineering

Deliverables

  • Pilot charter, roles, governance, timeline, and escalation path
  • Backlog, dependencies, risks, decisions, and change control
  • User onboarding plan, feedback process, and issue triage
  • KPI tracking and adoption evidence
  • Operating handoff and executive scale/stop/revise brief

Quoted separately: additional sites, business units, workflows, or user groups; software engineering; 24/7 support; security certification; legal advice; licenses; cloud costs; travel; and third-party vendor fees.

Commercial guardrails

What every proposal will make explicit.

  • Payment: staged fixed-fee work generally uses 50% to schedule and begin, with the balance tied to the agreed midpoint or final delivery. Monthly leadership is paid at the start of each service month.
  • Client inputs: the client provides timely access to accountable people, representative data, systems, policies, and decisions.
  • Change control: new workflows, integrations, datasets, sites, business units, or stakeholder groups trigger written scope review.
  • POC boundary: a prototype demonstrates feasibility; it is not automatically secure, scalable, maintained, compliant, or production-ready.
  • Third-party costs: software, API usage, cloud hosting, travel, specialist review, and licenses are separate unless listed.
  • Intellectual property: client-specific deliverables and PECULAB reusable methods, templates, and teaching materials are distinguished in writing.
  • Acceptance: each proposal identifies review dates, acceptance criteria, feedback windows, and what constitutes completion.
