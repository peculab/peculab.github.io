Workshop outcomes / July 5, 2026 / Bellevue, WA

AI-Era Parent-Child Co-Learning: Tools Change, Relationships Accumulate.

This was the first public PecuLab parent-child AI co-learning workshop. Families explored how AI can move from a tool that produces answers to a partner that helps children observe needs, compare choices, refine prompts, and turn family conversations into visible work.

Download Slides View Student Work

Interactive anchor

Parent-Child Future Design: What Could Change by 2036?

A core workshop activity asked families to look at today's friction and imagine what could change by 2036. The prompt was intentionally practical: identify a need, talk through what could become easier, safer, more personal, or more meaningful, and then use AI to help visualize the idea.

The works below show that AI literacy is not only about operating a tool. It is about learning how to ask better questions together, connect technology to real human needs, and keep the child and parent in the design loop.

Participant 1 / Image 01

From sketching travel steps to designing assistance

The hand drawing breaks a future travel experience into small scenes: tickets, maps, timing, family movement, and assisted transportation.

It connects to the 2036 activity by turning "what could change?" into parent-child decisions: what is stressful today, what should AI notice, and where should a helpful travel companion step in?

Participant 1 / Image 02

A smart travel companion for family planning

The AI-generated poster imagines trip planning, route adjustment, weather response, and autonomous luggage as a connected family travel system.

The piece extends the slide discussion by showing how a family idea can become a structured storyboard: AI compares options, explains tradeoffs, and supports shared decision-making.

Participant 2

Four hopes for a healthier 2036

This collage imagines disease cures, climate recovery, deep-space travel, and healthy affordable food as everyday possibilities.

It answers the 2036 design prompt at a systems level, moving from personal wishes into public health, environment, exploration, and daily wellbeing.

Participant 3 / Image 01

Transportation across time

The sketch compares boats, early aircraft, today's airplanes, and future airport robots and shuttles.

It makes 2036 visible as a timeline, placing future AI travel inside a longer history of solving distance, speed, safety, and coordination problems.

Participant 3 / Image 02

2036 travel as smarter, safer, and more caring

The final poster contrasts ancient boats, early airplanes, today's airports, and a 2036 AI-supported travel environment.

The result highlights a key lecture point: AI should not only make things faster. In parent-child design, "better" also means safer, clearer, and more considerate.

Participant 4

A future itinerary that cares for the whole day

This storyboard includes exploring places, managing jet lag, arranging transportation and schedules, eating organic food, sleeping better, and getting help during the trip.

It captures the parent-child emphasis especially well: future design is not one gadget, but a family routine where AI reduces friction while people still choose what matters.

Participant 5

Customer service that remembers and reduces waiting

The poster starts with today's frustration: appointments, calls, queues, repeated information, and long wait times. It then imagines AI agents that know preferences and solve problems faster.

This mirrors the interactive slide: begin with a need today, name the inconvenience, and design what could change by 2036 through saved time, lower stress, and personal support.

Participant 6 / Image 01

Needs first: time, effort, family, money, creativity

This image lists the needs AI might meet today: saving time, reducing effort, reducing manpower, increasing holidays, increasing income, and supporting creativity.

It is the planning layer behind the 2036 prompt. Before asking AI to make a picture, the participant identified the human value the future design should serve.

Participant 6 / Image 02

Today's inconvenience becomes the design brief

The second image names traffic jams, long working hours, lack of family time, and too much work pressure as problems to redesign.

This is the parent-child design method from the slides: describe the real-life pain point first, then let AI help explore how the future could respond to it.

Participant 6 / Image 03

AI change as life and work upgrade

This concept board translates problems into outcomes: saving time, reducing pressure, increasing creativity, increasing income, and enjoying life with family and self.

The piece reinforces that AI adoption should be judged by human outcomes. A good prompt is anchored in what kind of life the family wants to protect.

Participant 6 / Image 04

Two work days, five days to live

The poster imagines AI handling analysis, content generation, meeting summaries, and project management so people can work fewer days and center leisure, family, interests, and creativity.

It makes the 2036 question vivid: if AI changes productivity, what should people do with the time it returns? Here the answer is a richer family and personal life.

Participant 6 / Image 05

Prompt refinement through visual comparison

This alternate poster keeps the same central idea while changing composition, emphasis, and supporting details around work automation and family-centered rest.

The pair of P6-04 and P6-05 shows an important AI learning behavior: students can compare versions, notice what communicates better, and refine the prompt instead of accepting the first output.

What the outcomes show

The strongest results came from conversation, not from automation alone.

Across the works, families connected AI to concrete needs: travel planning, customer service, health, climate, food, work pressure, time, and family life.

PecuLab's workshop goal is to help families build that habit together: observe, discuss, prompt, compare, revise, and explain. The visible artifact is a picture, but the deeper outcome is a shared language for using AI with care.
