Summer AI Project Studio

From idea to working AI project.

A small-group project studio for high school students who want to explore AI, Python, Data Science, and Computer Vision through real projects.

This is not a traditional tutoring program or a homework-completion service. Students work in small teams to define a problem, collect or prepare data, build with Python and AI tools, test their ideas, and present a working project.

Students May Build

  • AI chatbot or assistant
  • Data science and visualization project
  • Machine learning mini-project
  • Computer vision prototype
  • Science fair, capstone, independent project, or portfolio-ready demo

Designed For

High school students who are curious about AI but need guidance turning an idea into a clear, achievable project.

Format

Small group | 3-5 students | Online | Chinese / English / bilingual support | Summer pilot cohort

Goal

Help students build something they understand, can demonstrate, and can explain with confidence.

Studio Journey

  1. Discover: choose a meaningful problem and define what success looks like.
  2. Plan: break the idea into data, model, interface, testing, and presentation tasks.
  3. Build: use Python, notebooks, AI tools, APIs, datasets, or vision libraries to create a prototype.
  4. Explain: prepare a demo, project story, limitations, and next-step plan.

What Students Receive

  • A scoped project proposal
  • A working prototype or analysis notebook
  • A short technical write-up
  • A final demo presentation
  • Portfolio-ready project language they can explain honestly

About

A new chapter in AI education, project-based learning, and purposeful technology.

My work has always lived between teaching and building.

Before becoming a university professor, I worked in software engineering, financial technology, quantitative finance, and applied computing. Later, across appointments at National Taiwan University, Soochow University, and National Taiwan Normal University, I designed courses and learning experiences that helped students move from concepts to working systems.

After relocating to Kirkland, Washington with my family, I began thinking more deeply about the next chapter of my work: how to make AI education more practical, personal, and meaningful for students, families, and professionals.

I believe AI should not only make people faster. It should help them learn better, think more clearly, build meaningful projects, and create more freedom in life.

My current focus is helping learners turn curiosity into capability and ideas into working AI projects.

Teaching & Mentoring Approach

From concept to implementation.

Helping learners build systems they can explain, not just code that runs.

Builder's Mindset

Students should be able to define the problem, explain assumptions, justify design choices, and improve what they build.

AI With Clear Thinking

In the generative AI era, students must do more than prompt a model. They should learn to direct the technology, critique its limitations, and build original solutions.

Inclusive High Standards

My teaching relies on co-learning communities, after-class support, shared repositories, peer learning, and direct mentoring rather than lowering expectations.
