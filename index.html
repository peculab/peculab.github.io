About

A clear academic story with a builder’s mindset

My work sits at the intersection of teaching, curriculum design, applied AI, and educational scholarship. Across appointments at NTNU, Soochow University, and National Taiwan University, I have consistently designed learning experiences that move students from concept to implementation.

Academic Profile

I teach programming, machine learning, data analytics, intelligent systems, and project-based computing courses. My approach emphasizes authentic datasets, interpretable models, and working prototypes rather than abstract exercises alone.

In the generative AI era, I believe students must do more than prompt a model. They should learn to direct the technology, critique its limitations, justify their decisions, and build original solutions.

Teaching

From concept to implementation

My teaching philosophy is simple: a system is not finished just because the code runs. Students should be able to explain assumptions, justify design choices, and improve what they build.

Teaching Philosophy

I emphasize a builder’s mindset. In my classroom, students use AI as a tool for inquiry and creation, not as a shortcut around thinking. I want them to direct AI, critique its limits, and turn ideas into functional solutions.

Course & Curriculum Design

As co-PI and collaborative designer of NTNU’s Educational Big Data Micro Program, I helped build a structured pathway from foundational learning to advanced analytics and then to practice-oriented implementation.

The program explicitly asked students to understand educational big data, conduct analysis, apply appropriate models, and create MVPs or proofs of concept.

Scaffolding & Inclusive Teaching

Inclusive teaching means building structures that help more students meet high standards. My teaching relies on co-learning communities, after-class support, shared repositories, peer learning, and direct mentoring rather than lowering expectations.
